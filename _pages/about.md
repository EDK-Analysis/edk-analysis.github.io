I am a Research Associate (staff scientist) at the [Cornell High Energy Synchrotron Source (CHESS)](https://www.chess.cornell.edu/), part of the Cornell Laboratory for Accelerator-Based Sciences and Education (CLASSE). My work centers on time-resolved synchrotron X-ray experiments and the insights they offer into materials science. I currently study electrically driven phase transitions using time-resolved X-ray scattering, and the reaction dynamics of electro-catalysts using time-resolved X-ray spectroscopy. I also design and build time-resolved X-ray detectors for synchrotron science.

Formerly, I was a postdoctoral researcher in the Resonant Inelastic X-ray Scattering group at the [Advanced Light Source (Lawrence Berkeley National Lab)](https://als.lbl.gov/), advised by Dr. Jinghua Guo, and before that a postdoc for Dr. Xiaoyi Zhang at the [Advanced Photon Source (Argonne National Lab)](https://www.aps.anl.gov/). I earned my Ph.D. in Physics from Columbia University in 2018.

You can find my full CV [here](/files/cv_kinigstein.pdf).
