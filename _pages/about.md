I am a Research Associate (staff scientist) at the [Cornell High Energy Synchrotron Source (CHESS)](https://www.chess.cornell.edu/), part of the Cornell Laboratory for Accelerator-Based Sciences and Education (CLASSE). My work centers on time-resolved synchrotron X-ray experiments and the insights they offer into materials science. I currently study electrically driven phase transitions using time-resolved X-ray scattering, and the reaction dynamics of electro-catalysts using time-resolved X-ray spectroscopy. I also design and build time-resolved X-ray detectors for synchrotron science.

Formerly, I was a postdoctoral researcher in the Resonant Inelastic X-ray Scattering group at the [Advanced Light Source (Lawrence Berkeley National Lab)](https://als.lbl.gov/), advised by Dr. Jinghua Guo, where I integrated electrochemical measurements with time-resolved soft X-ray spectroscopy and studied gas diffusion electrodes for electrochemical H₂O₂ generation. Before that, I worked as a postdoc for Dr. Xiaoyi Zhang at the [Advanced Photon Source (Argonne National Lab)](https://www.aps.anl.gov/), developing time-resolved hard X-ray spectroscopy techniques and studying photochemical reaction mechanisms.

I earned my Ph.D. in Physics from Columbia University in 2018, where my thesis work focused on the structural and optical characterization of solution-processed lead iodide Ruddlesden-Popper perovskite thin films.

I was recently awarded an **NSF ACCESS Explore allocation** to support my computational work.

I am currently seeking collaborators who focus on the synthesis and characterization of transition metal oxide thin films, with an emphasis on combinatorial techniques. Please feel free to reach out via email (edk59 at cornell dot edu) if you would like to schedule a meeting.
